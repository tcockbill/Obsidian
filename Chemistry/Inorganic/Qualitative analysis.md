# Quantitative vs Qualitative  
Quantitative -> based on measurements/numerical results (E.g. Titration)  
Qualitative -> based on observations (E.g. Squeaky pop test)  
  
# Testing for [[../../To do/Carbonate ions|carbonate ions]] $(CO_3\space^{2-})$  
1. Add acid $(H^+)$ to the sample  
2. If bubbling is seen, the sample could comtain [[../../To do/Carbonate ions|carbonate ions]]  
$MgCO_3 + 2HCl \rightarrow MgCl_2 + CO_2 + H_2O$  
3. Confirm presence of [[../../To do/Carbonate ions|carbonate ions]] by bubbling the gas through limewater (saturated solution of $Ca(OH)_2$) - if positive, limewater turns cloudy  
$Ca(OH)_{2(aq)} + CO_{2(g)} \rightarrow CaCO_{3(s)} + H_2O_{(l)}$  
  
# Testing for [[../../To do/Sulphate ions|sulphate ions]] $(SO_4 \space ^{2-})$  
1. Add aqueous barium chloride (or barium nitrate) to the sample  
2. If [[../../To do/Sulphate ions|sulphate ions]] are present, a white [[../../To do/Precipitate|ppt]] of barium sulphate will be seen  
$Ba^{2+}\space_{(aq)} + SO_4^{2-}\space _{(aq)} \rightarrow BaSO_{4(s)}$  
  
# Testing for [[../Definitions/Halogens|halide ions]] $(X^-)$  
While most halides are soluble in water, silver halides for precipitates  
$$Ag^+_{(aq)}+X^-_{(aq)} \rightarrow AgX_{(s)}$$  
1. Add aqueous $AgNO_3$ to a solution of the halide ion  
2. identify the halide ion present by the colour of the [[../../To do/Precipitate|ppt]] formed  
	- $Cl=white$, $Br=cream$, $I=yellow$  
3. Add aqueous $NH_3$ to test the solubility of the ppts - an important step as the colours can be hard to distinguish  
	- $Cl$ = dissolves with dilute $NH_3$, $Br$ = dissolves with concentrtated $NH_3$, $I$ = remains insoluble  
  
# Testing order  
Carbonate -> Sulphate -> Halide  
## Carbonate  
Carbonate first as sulphate and halides do not bubble  
Use nitric acid to avoid introducing chloride or [[../../To do/Sulphate ions|sulphate ions]] and keep adding until bubbling stops  
  
## Sulphate  
$BaCO_3$ is also white and insoluble, so the carbonate test must be performed first to rule out the presence of [[../../To do/Carbonate ions|carbonate ions]]  
Use barium nitrate to avoid introducing chloride ions, and filter off ppt before performing the test for halide ions  
  
## Halide  
Silver carbonate and silver sulphate are both insoluble and will form ppts, meaning their presence must be ruled out before performing the halide tests  
  
  
  
|               | With silver nitrate              | With ammonia                                                         |  
| ------------- | -------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |

### Equations  
#### With silver nitrate  
$Ag^+ (aq) + Cl^- (aq) \rightarrow AgCl(s)$  
$Ag^+ (aq) + Br^- (aq) \rightarrow AgBr(s)$  
$Ag^+ (aq) + I^- (aq) \rightarrow AgI(s)$
